The PCSO 4-Digit Game

On August 1997, the Philippine Charity Sweepstakes Office (PCSO) launched the 4-Digit Game as the first digit game in the Philippines.

The 4-Digit Game is played three times weekly, every monday, wednesday and friday.

How to play 4-Digit Game?

Playing 4-Digit Game is simple. Go to any authorized PCSO lotto outlet and get a 4-Digit Game playslip and mark your chosen four (4) numbers (each digit from numbers 0 to 9) you can choose four same numbers (Example: 5-5-5-5 ). After that submit your playslip to the outlet operator and wait for your ticket. You will pay ₱12 for every standard play.

You can also play the different System plays.

How does 4-Digit Game different system play works?

4-Digit Game | Roll 1
In this system, you only need to choose 3 numbers for the last 3 digits. You don't need to worry about your first digit, it's already bonus for you. That means you have ten 4 digit combinations

Example: If your chosen numbers for the second, third and last digit are _-5-6-7

By playing Roll 1 your combinations will be: -5-6-7

4-Digit Game | Roll 4
Similar to Roll 1 except that in this system, you will only choose the first three digits and your last digit is bonus. That means you have ten 4 digit combinations

Example: If your chosen numbers for the first, second and third digit are 5-5-6-_

By playing Roll 4 your combinations will be: 5-5-6-

4-Digit Game | Perm or Rambol
The system will automatically combine all the different possible combinations of your chosen 4 numbers. Giving you more number combinations.

If your chosen number combination is composed of four (4) different numbers, example: 0-1-2-3 the system can generate a twenty four (24) combinations.

If your chosen number combination is composed of 2 different number and 1 pair of the same number, example: 0-1-2-2 the system can generate a twelve (12) combinations.

If your chosen number combination is composed of 2 different pairs of the same numbers, example: 0-0-1-1 the system can generate a six (6) combinations.

If your chosen number combination is composed of 3 digits the same and 1 different, example: 0-0-0-1 the system can generate a four (4) combinations.

How to win in 4-Digit Game?

To win the 1st prize, you need to match correctly all four of the winning numbers in exact order. You can also win prizes by getting the last three or last two of the winning numbers, in exact order.

How much can I win?

If you got the perfect four of the winning number drawn, in exact order, you win the 1st prize, Minimum Guaranteed Amount (MGA) of ₱10,000. If you got the last three digits you can win (MGA) ₱800. (MGA) ₱100 if you got the last two digits. Take a look at the prize breakdown table of each draw to see what you’ve won.

What if there are more than one first prize winner?

The 4-Digit Game is a pari-mutuel game. Meaning if there are more than one first prize winner, the prize money will be divided equally among the winners.

The 1st prize pool is not snowballed/carried over to the next draw. Whatever is the pool generated for the 1st Prize is equally divided among the number of 1st prize winners for that particular draw.

Where can I see the 4-Digit Game results?

The draws is televise in PTV 4. You can also find the official and verified 4-Digit Game results right here on the LottoInfo.ph website. Just head to the 4-Digit Game draw results page. The winning numbers are posted once the draw results have been verified and confirmed. However, we still encourage you to visit the official PCSO website if you still want to verify the game results there.

The draws are held at 9:00 p.m. every mondays, wednesdays and fridays.

How do I claim the money if I win?

Claiming your 4-Digit Game winnings is easy. If you win prizes amounting to ₱10,000 and below you can claim it at any authorized Lotto outlet or at the PCSO branch offices near you. If you win more than ₱10,000 you can claim it at any of the PCSO branch offices or at the PCSO head office, located at 605 Conservatory Bldg., Shaw Blvrd. Corner Princeton St., Mandaluyong city.
